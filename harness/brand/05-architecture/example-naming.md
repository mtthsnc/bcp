Northwind is a **branded house**: every product carries the Northwind master brand first, followed by a plain-language product name. Format: `Northwind <Product>` (e.g. *Northwind Ridgeline Tent*, *Northwind Trailhead Pack*).

Naming rules:
- Product names are descriptive, not invented words. Favor real outdoor terms (Ridgeline, Trailhead, Summit) over coined names.
- No sub-brands, no nested ™ stacks. There is one trademark: **Northwind™**, marked on first use per page only.
- Product lines, not models, get names. Variants are described, not named: *Northwind Ridgeline Tent (2-person)*, never *Ridgeline Tent X2*.
- Capitalize the master brand and product name; lowercase generic category words (`Northwind Ridgeline Tent`, the `tent`).

**Why:** A single, plainly-named house lets the master brand accrue all trust and recognition, keeps the catalog legible to customers, and removes the ongoing cost of inventing, clearing, and protecting many separate marks.
