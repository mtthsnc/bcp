# 02 — Voice

How the brand **sounds**. Voice is the consistent personality; tone flexes by context. This section also holds **refusals** — the things the brand will never say or do.

## What goes here
- **Tone** — the voice's personality and how it adapts (`type: truth`)
- **Lexicon** — words we use, words we avoid, spelling and capitalization conventions
- **Do / Say** — concrete phrasing guidance with examples
- **Refusals** — never-say / never-do entries (`type: refusal`)

## Refusals are first-class
A refusal is a hard boundary, not a preference. They live here as truth files with `type: refusal`. Good refusals are specific and enforceable ("we never use fear to sell") so that any reviewer — or model — can catch a violation. They are a signature BCP idea: defining what you'll never do is as load-bearing as defining what you will.

## How to author
- One concern per file; all files carry the truth frontmatter contract.
- Show, don't just tell — pair guidance with a short before/after or example.
- Always include the `**Why:**` line.
