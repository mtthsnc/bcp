# 0001 — Shift voice from playful to confident-plain

**Decision:** Northwind's voice moves from *playful and pun-heavy* to **confident and plain**. We keep warmth and the occasional dry aside, but drop wordplay, exclamation points, and hype adjectives ("epic", "ultimate", "game-changing").

**Rationale:**
- Loop data (`09-loops/example-loop-2026-06.md`) showed the playful register scored low for trust on gear-spec and safety content, where customers want directness.
- Confident-plain reads as expertise — fitting for a brand selling equipment people rely on outdoors.
- It is easier to apply consistently across agents and contexts than a humor-dependent voice, reducing brand drift.

**Alternatives considered:**
- *Keep playful, restrict to marketing surfaces* — rejected; the split confused agents and produced inconsistent output.
- *Go fully formal/technical* — rejected; loses the warmth that differentiates Northwind.

**Effect:** Amends the voice truths in `02-voice`. Supersedes any prior "playful tone" guidance. Adds the refusal "no hype adjectives" (to be authored in `02`/refusals).
