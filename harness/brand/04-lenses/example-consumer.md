**Context:** Direct-to-consumer web — product pages, email, onboarding. Audience is the individual hiker researching a purchase.

**Emphasize:** durability and the lifetime repair program (`example-positioning`); published failure data as proof; the warm end of our tone range (`example-tone`).

**Soft-pedal:** procurement/volume language and certification minutiae — relevant to B2B buyers, noise here.

**Adjustments within range:**
- Lead with the human outcome ("repair it, don't replace it"), then back it with the number.
- Use `color.trail` for a single primary CTA per view; keep the page calm.
- Second person, active voice. Still no hype — refusals (`example-refusals`) apply unchanged.

**Why:** The consumer is buying for themselves and rewards proof and plain talk. This lens surfaces the truths that matter to them and dials tone warmer, without bending a single underlying claim.
